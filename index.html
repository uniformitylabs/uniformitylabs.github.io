    3D Printed Materials in Mass Production
UNIFORMITY LABS is an additive manufacturing (AM) parts, printing, and metal feedstock producer that has pioneered revolutionary refining and printing processes that significantly enhance the 3D printing value proposition. Our feedstock materials and print processes have a dramatic impact throughout the AM value chain by increasing the speed, quality, reliability, efficiency, and economics of printing. Uniformity product offerings consist of powders and parts for heavy equipment manufacturers (OEM), automotive manufacturers, consumer electronics companies, aerospace suppliers, energy generation and oil & gas, the U.S. federal government, and other market participants in the global AM industry.

Disruptively Advanced: Ultra Low Porosity Feedstock

- Full suite of metal materials and proprietary alloys for powder bed fusion, jetting and other platforms
- Segmented material grades offered to suit customer cost and quality preferences
Uniformity powders attain high relative densities, approaching that of the densest crystalline particle packings, while retaining their ability to flow without phase separation. Uniformity’s powder is comprised of an optimized maximally random jammed packing of particle sizes that:
- Exhibit negligible phase separation
- Maintain low viscosity / high fluidity
- Possess increased contact points between particles
- Resulting porosity is reduced to 15% - 25% (compared to 40% - 50%)

Innovative fabrication technology and software

- Small batch production of printed parts at Uniformity
- Part redesign, optimization for AM
- UL software and print demonstrations, training and design for AM
- Printer selection, printer setup with Uniformity powder and print processes

Powder Bed Fusion Benefits

- 3-10x increased printer throughput due to low porosity powder
- Reduced use of support structures → faster printing and less finishing required
- More reliable printing due to less warpage and residual stress
- Reduced material usage and waste
- No impact to powder recycling

Binder Jetting Benefits

- 6% - 9% linear shrinkage and 99.5% final part density
- 20%-30% increased printer throughput due to higher use of build volume (reduced shrinkage) and faster powder spreading
- Reduced use of binder
- More reliable printing due to less warpage and shrinkage
- No impact to powder recycling

Breakthrough technology unlocks the full potential of AM

Founded in 2014 and based in Fremont, CA, Uniformity Labs (UL) develops breakthrough material and software solutions that aim to accelerate and expand global commercial and industrial 3D printing (Additive Manufacturing, AM) markets. The company’s patent-pending technology enables significant cost savings, speed & quality improvements across all mainstream AM printers. Uniformity’s feedstock materials and print processes have a dramatic impact throughout the AM value chain by increasing reliability and efficiency of printing.

Refined feedstock and processes yields significant and measurable improvements in several key properties of the end product. Additive Manufacturing powder is half the cost to produce relative to other AM powders. Uniformity’s technology and raw materials enable printers to operate at significantly faster speeds, with increased reliability and decreased finishing. Powder can be made from any metal or non-metal material. Powder and processes work on all major printing platforms

Uniformity’s powder, printing and software services achieves significant improvements in all additive manufacturing areas, which will accelerate the growth of the entire market.

The global AM market is enormous and largely unpenetrated, with extraordinary long-term growth prospects and mass adoption potential. AM is rapidly displacing conventional manufacturing, which historically has been less efficient, more costly, and generated parts of comparatively lower quality limited by geometric constraints
