UNIFORMITY LABS is an additive manufacturing (AM) parts, printing, and metal feedstock producer that has pioneered revolutionary refining and printing processes that significantly enhance the 3D printing value proposition. Our feedstock materials and print processes have a dramatic impact throughout the AM value chain by increasing the speed, quality, reliability, efficiency, and economics of printing.
Uniformity provides a full suite of metal materials and proprietary alloys for powder bed fusion, jetting and other platforms and segmented material grades. Uniformity powders attain high relative densities, approaching that of the densest crystalline particle packings, while retaining their ability to flow without phase separation:
• Exhibits negligible phase separation and maintains low viscosity / high fluidity
• Possesses increased contact points between particles
• Resulting porosity is reduced to 15% - 25% (compared to 40% - 50%)

Powder Bed Fusion Benefits

• 3-10x increased printer throughput due to low porosity powder
• Reduced use of support structures → faster printing and less finishing required
• More reliable printing due to less warpage and residual stress
• Reduced material usage and waste
• No impact to powder recycling

Binder Jetting Benefits

• 6% - 9% linear shrinkage and 99.5% final part density
• 20%-30% increased printer throughput due to higher use of build volume (reduced shrinkage) and faster powder spreading
• No impact to powder recycling and reduced use of binder
• More reliable printing due to less warpage and shrinkage

Uniformity Labs (UL), founded in 2014 and based in Fremont CA, develops breakthrough material and software solutions that aim to accelerate and expand global commercial and industrial 3D printing (Additive Manufacturing, AM) markets. The company’s patent-pending technology enables significant cost savings, speed & quality improvements across all mainstream AM printers. Uniformity’s feedstock materials and print processes have a dramatic impact throughout the AM value chain by increasing reliability and efficiency of printing.

The global AM market is enormous and largely unpenetrated, with extraordinary long-term growth prospects and mass adoption potential. AM is rapidly displacing conventional manufacturing, which historically has been less efficient, more costly, and generated parts of comparatively lower quality limited by geometric constraints
